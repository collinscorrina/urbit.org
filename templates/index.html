This is a drawing of an Urbit.

It’s a new kind of computer.

This image is representative of three key (yet familiar) aspects to this new kind of computer:

  • The apps you use every day,
  • the OS that makes sure everything runs smoothly,
  • and your identity at the center of it all.

So, what is an Urbit?

Nothing quite like this has ever been done before, and nothing is quite like it.

Today, technology controls us.

Every MEGACORP has the same incentive:
To keep us hooked on their platform.

We think that communities of people should decide their own structure, which means they also have to control their own history and the means by which they write into it. We think that history should be owned by the people creating it, not by some external faction. Without control over the stories we tell one another, we’re ruled by someone else.

We built Urbit so people can control their technology, shape their digital lives and freely build their digital communities.

We want to replace the internet with a foundation for computing that we can rely on far into the future.

Urbit is a large, ambitious project.

But at the core it’s durable, simple and yours.
